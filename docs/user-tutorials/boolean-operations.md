# Boolean Operations

*Merge and carve shapes to build new ones.*

## Use case
Boolean operations combine overlapping shapes into brand-new forms. Fuse two
circles into a cloud, punch a hole through a square, or keep only the overlap. The
fastest way to make custom icons and logos from simple shapes.

## Relevant for
- Two or more overlapping shapes selected together.
- Custom icons, logos, and complex outlines.

## How to test it
1. Draw two overlapping shapes and select both.
2. Click **Union** — they fuse into one shape.
3. Undo, then click **Subtract** — the top shape cuts a hole in the bottom one.
4. Try **Intersect** to keep only the overlapping area.

## Example — hollow circle (donut / wheel)

Draw a large circle (outer edge), then draw a smaller circle centered on top (inner edge).
Select both and click **Subtract** — the inner circle is cut away, leaving a true annulus
with an outline on both the outer and inner edges. Use this for wheels, rings, or any shape
that needs a transparent hole.

## Related properties
- **Union** — merge into one shape.
- **Intersect** — keep only where they overlap.
- **Subtract** — remove the top shape from the bottom.
- **Exclude** — keep everything except the overlap.
- **Divide** — split the bottom shape into separate pieces along the top one.
