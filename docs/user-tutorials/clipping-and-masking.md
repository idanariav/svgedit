# Clipping & Masking

*Show an object only through a shape you choose.*

## Use case
Clipping and masking let one shape control what you can see of another. Crop a
photo into a circle, reveal artwork through letters, or fade an image at its edges.
A clip makes a hard cut; a mask allows soft, gradual fades. Feathering softens the
edge for a gentle vignette or glow.

## Relevant for
- Exactly two objects: the shape on top defines what shows through the one below.
- Cropping photos, framed reveals, and soft-edged effects.

## How to test it
1. Place a shape (the "window") on top of an image or object.
2. Select both.
3. Click **Set Clip Path** — the lower object shows only inside the top shape.
4. Undo and try **Set Mask** for a soft, see-through reveal.
5. Adjust **Feather** to soften the edge, or click **Release** to undo the effect.

## Related properties
- **Set Clip Path** — hard crop to the top shape.
- **Set Mask** — soft, brightness-based reveal.
- **Feather** — soften the edge (turns a clip into a mask automatically).
- **Release** — remove the clip or mask.
