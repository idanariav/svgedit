# Ellipse & Circle

*Dots, bubbles, pies, and perfect circles.*

## Use case
Draw ellipses and circles for buttons, avatars, status dots, and bubbles. Turn a
circle into a pie slice or arc by lowering its arc span — handy for charts,
gauges, and badges.

## Relevant for
- Starting a new round shape on the canvas.
- Any design needing dots, rings, pies, or rounded accents.

## How to test it
1. Click the Ellipse tool, or press **E**.
2. Drag on the canvas to draw an ellipse.
3. Click and hold the tool to reveal its variants: **Circle** and **Freehand Ellipse**.
4. Pick **Circle** and drag — it stays perfectly round.
5. Select a circle and lower its **Arc** value below 360° — it becomes a pie slice.

## Related properties
- **Center X / Center Y** — position of the center.
- **Radius** (circle) or **Horizontal/Vertical radius** (ellipse) — size.
- **Arc** — span in degrees; under 360° turns the circle into a wedge.
- **Variants** — Circle, Freehand Ellipse.
