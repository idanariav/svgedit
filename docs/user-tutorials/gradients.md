# Gradients

*Blend colors smoothly for depth and shine.*

## Use case
A gradient fades one color into another for a rich, polished look. Use linear
gradients for backgrounds and buttons, radial gradients for glows and spheres, and
multiple stops for sunset skies or metallic shine. Add depth that a flat color
can't.

## Relevant for
- The fill (or stroke) of any shape.
- Backgrounds, buttons, badges, and glossy effects.

## How to test it
1. Select a shape and open the **Fill** color picker.
2. Switch the fill type to a gradient (linear or radial).
3. Drag the color stops to change where colors blend.
4. Click a stop to change its color, and add stops for more colors.

## Related properties
- **Gradient type** — linear (straight blend) or radial (from a center).
- **Color stops** — the colors along the blend and where they sit.
- Applies to **fill** and **stroke** (see Fill & Stroke Color).
